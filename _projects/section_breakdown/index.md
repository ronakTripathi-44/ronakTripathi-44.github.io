## Project Overview - Sectional Representation and Visual Communication Workflow
This project focuses on the role of sectional representation as a precise instrument of architectural documentation and communication within a BIM-based planning environment. Using an open-source residential Revit model, I developed a structured workflow for generating presentation-quality sectional views that meet professional documentation standards. The objective was not to alter the architectural design, but to refine the graphical articulation of the model in a way that aligns with the expectations of contemporary planning practice.

Within the framework of the HOAI Leistungsphasen, sectional clarity becomes increasingly relevant from Entwurfsplanung (LPH 3) through Ausführungsplanung (LPH 5). Sections are essential for communicating spatial relationships, structural systems, and envelope composition to consultants, clients, and approval authorities. They serve as a bridge between conceptual intent and technical execution. This project investigates how a BIM model can be systematically controlled to produce sections that are not merely automatic outputs, but carefully curated documents.

The workflow emphasizes deliberate section placement to reveal structural grids, circulation systems, and vertical spatial sequences. Graphic overrides, visibility controls, and view templates were applied to establish hierarchy and reduce visual noise. Particular attention was given to lineweight control, depth differentiation, and material cut representation in order to achieve legibility without overcomplication. The result is a series of sectional views suitable for brochures, portfolio presentation, and internal design review contexts.

In practice, the quality of representation directly influences project communication and coordination efficiency. Clear sectional drawings improve interdisciplinary alignment and reduce ambiguity during later planning phases. This project demonstrates an understanding of BIM not only as a modeling tool, but as a structured documentation environment capable of producing consistent and high-quality visual outputs.

While Revit’s internal rendering capabilities were evaluated as part of the workflow, it is acknowledged that high-resolution visualization for external communication often requires supplementary tools. The integrated Twinmotion interface offers a viable extension for achieving presentation-grade imagery when required. Nevertheless, the primary focus of this work remains the disciplined production of architectural documentation within the BIM authoring environment itself.
